About Us

About GPs

GPs are not specialised, rather than reading on book on a shelf, they take a page out of all of them.

They treat common and refer cases to specialists if nessasary. They focus on the overall health of a person from physical to mental health. Your GP always has you covered and if he doesn't, they'll know someone who does.

• Patient consultations at home and within the surgery.
• Physical examinations.
• Diagnosis and treatment of illnesses.
• Working with other healthcare professionals and/or hospitals.

What GPs do

GPs attend to patients of all ages from newborn babies to elderly people. The continuous relationship that GPs are able to establish with their patients and being able to offer continuity of care is one of the most paramount and enjoyable aspects of the job.

Patients are often quite well informed about their own conditions and GPs will work with them together to decide on a mutually acceptable plan.
